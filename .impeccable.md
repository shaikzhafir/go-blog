## Design Context

### Users
Broad audience visiting a personal blog — developers, readers, friends, and curious strangers. They arrive from search, social media, or direct links. Context: browsing casually on laptop or phone, looking for interesting writing about books, coding, travel, manga, and running.

### Brand Personality
Casual, nerdy, warm. The blog should feel like visiting a friend's well-organized bookshelf — personal, approachable, and a little quirky. Not corporate, not trying too hard.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Clean and editorial with warmth. Inspired by wo-df-ir.com — generous whitespace, confident typography, content-first layout.
- **Anti-references**: Generic developer portfolio sites, dark-mode-everything dashboards, overly polished SaaS landing pages.
- **Theme**: Light mode (intentional choice).
- **Colors**: Open to experimentation. Current green accent and slate background are not precious.

### Design Principles
1. **Content is king** — The writing and content should be the star, not the chrome around it.
2. **Warm, not sterile** — Subtle personality through typography, color, and small details rather than flashy effects.
3. **Readable first** — Typography and spacing should make long-form reading effortless.
4. **Honest and personal** — Should feel like a person made this, not a template or AI.
5. **Light and airy** — Generous whitespace, breathing room, no visual clutter.
